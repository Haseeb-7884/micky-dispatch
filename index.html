FAQs

A dispatcher can significantly improve your company's performance by finding high-paying loads, negotiating better rates, and managing logistics efficiently. They can also help with back-office tasks, such as billing and driver management, allowing you to focus on driving and growing your business

Dispatchers typically use load boards, like DAT, to find loads. They also leverage personal connections with freight brokers and shippers to secure high-paying freight. Some dispatchers may even use their own networks to find exclusive loads

The cost of a freight dispatch service varies. Some dispatchers charge a flat rate per load (around $50), while others take a percentage of the invoice (usually 5-10%). This percentage can add up quickly, especially for smaller operations

Reputable dispatch services often provide additional support, including:
  • Compliance Management: ensuring motor carrier compliance
  • Shipper Relations: managing relationships with shippers
  • Delay Management: handling unexpected delays
  • Back-Office Support: assisting with paperwork, billing, and collections

Dispatch services may specialize in various load types, such as:
  • Flatbed
  • LTL (Less Than Truckload)
  • Cargo Van
  • Specialty Loads

The number of trucks a dispatcher manages varies. Some dispatchers work with single owner-operators, while others manage larger fleets. Experienced dispatchers can handle multiple trucks, ensuring each truck is efficiently utilized
